MYTHOLOGIES DUEL - RULES EXTRACTED (ADAPTED FROM ORIGINAL STRUCTURE)

This variant preserves the core 3-Age drafting, construction, economy, science, chaining, and scoring logic of the original duel structure, while replacing Wonders and military pressure with a mythology-and-summoning system.

--------------------------------------------------
1. CORE IDEA
--------------------------------------------------
Each player leads a cultic network rather than a civilization.
Across 3 Ages, players construct Buildings exactly as in the base structure: resource production, commerce, science, civilian points, guilds, and chains all remain active.

Main thematic changes:
- Wonders are replaced by Gods / Cults.
- Military shields are replaced by Summoning points.
- Military supremacy is removed.
- Red cards no longer push a conflict marker. Instead they increase Summoning strength.
- At the end of each Age, a Summoning Resolution occurs.
- Winning a Summoning Resolution allows a player to activate Divine Powers from their built Gods.

Victory conditions become:
1. Scientific Supremacy (unchanged): if you gather 6 different scientific symbols, you win immediately.
2. Civilian Victory (adapted): if no supremacy occurs, total points at end of Age III.

--------------------------------------------------
2. COMPONENT CHANGES
--------------------------------------------------
Keep:
- Age I, II, III cards
- Guild cards
- Progress tokens
- Coins
- Board/card structures

Replace:
- 12 Wonders -> 16 God cards (4 mythologies x 4 gods each)
- Conflict pawn / military track -> Summoning track or simply a visible Summoning tally area
- Military tokens -> optional Summoning bonus markers (see section 8)

Resources remain mechanically identical:
- Sacred Timber
- Primordial Clay
- Ancient Stone
- Crystal Relic
- Sacred Scroll
You may optionally rename them thematically in presentation, but their logic is unchanged.

Suggested flavor renames:
- Wood -> Sacred Timber
- Clay -> Primordial Clay
- Stone -> Ancient Stone
- Glass -> Crystal Relic
- Papyrus -> Sacred Scroll

--------------------------------------------------
3. GOD SETS
--------------------------------------------------
There are 4 mythology families. Each family contains 4 possible gods.
The fourth slot in some mythologies is variable, as indicated below.

Greek
- Artemis
- Apollo
- Athena
- Zeus or Hera (randomly select one for the game)

Nordic
- Balder
- Tyr
- Odin
- Loki or Thor (randomly select one for the game)

Americas
- Huracán
- Bochica
- Great Spirit
- Quetzalcoatl or Inti (randomly select one for the game)

Mesopotamia
- Enki
- Enlil
- Marduk
- Anu

--------------------------------------------------
4. GOD SELECTION PHASE
--------------------------------------------------
Use a selection structure parallel to the original Wonder draft.

Setup:
- Before Age I, determine the active 16-god pool:
  - Greek: Artemis, Apollo, Athena, and randomly Zeus or Hera
  - Nordic: Balder, Tyr, Odin, and randomly Loki or Thor
  - Americas: Huracán, Bochica, Great Spirit, and randomly Quetzalcoatl or Inti
  - Mesopotamia: Enki, Enlil, Marduk, Anu
- Shuffle all 16 God cards.
- Reveal 4 Gods.
- First player chooses 1 God.
- Second player chooses 2 Gods.
- First player takes the remaining 1 God.
- Reveal 4 more Gods and repeat, but starting with the second player.

At the end of this phase, each player has 4 Gods available to build during the game.
Place them in a column beside your play area.

--------------------------------------------------
5. BUILDING GODS (REPLACES BUILDING WONDERS)
--------------------------------------------------
On your turn, instead of constructing the chosen Age card as a Building or discarding it for coins, you may use it to build one of your unbuilt Gods.

To build a God:
- Pay the resource cost of the God, not the cost of the chosen Age card.
- Place the Age card face down under the God to show it has been built.
- Apply that God's passive build effect immediately if it has one.
- The used Age card has no effect.
- Your 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th Gods require 1, 2, 4, and 6 built Buildings respectively before they may be completed.

God limits:
- Only 7 Gods may be built during a game total.
- The first player to build a 4th God may do so.
- Once one player has built 4 Gods, the other player may build only up to 3 Gods total.
- As soon as the 7th God is built, the last unbuilt God is removed from the game.

This preserves the asymmetry of the original 7-Wonder cap.

--------------------------------------------------
6. BUILDING COSTS, PRODUCTION, TRADING, CHAINS
--------------------------------------------------
These remain unchanged from the original system.

Unchanged mechanics:
- Free construction on cards that have no cost.
- Resource production from brown, grey, some yellow cards, and some God effects.
- Trading cost formula remains:
  COST = 2 + number of matching brown/grey resource symbols in the opposing city
- Yellow card discounts still work exactly the same.
- Chain construction still works exactly the same.
- Resources are not spent permanently; they are production capacity.

--------------------------------------------------
7. CARD COLOR FUNCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------
Brown cards: unchanged resource production.
Grey cards: unchanged manufactured goods production.
Blue cards: unchanged victory point buildings.
Green cards: unchanged science symbols and science pressure.
Yellow cards: unchanged coins / trade / flexible production logic.
Purple cards: unchanged guild scoring logic.
Red cards: transformed.

NEW RED CARD FUNCTION:
Red cards are now Invocation / Summoning Buildings.
- Each shield on a red card becomes 1 Summoning point.
- A red card with 2 shields gives 2 Summoning points.
- A red card with 3 shields gives 3 Summoning points.

Important:
- Red cards no longer move a conflict pawn.
- There is no military track.
- Red cards matter because they increase your power in end-of-Age Summoning Resolutions and may also score via adapted guild effects.

--------------------------------------------------
8. SUMMONING SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------
The military system is replaced by ritual competition between cults.

8.1 Summoning Value
Each player has a Summoning Value for the current Age:
Summoning Value = total Summoning points from:
- red cards in their city
- any built God that explicitly grants passive Summoning bonuses
- any Progress token or special effect that explicitly grants Summoning bonuses

Recommended balance rule:
- Count all currently built red cards each Age.
- Summoning is cumulative across the game, unless a card effect destroys a red card.

8.2 End of Age Summoning Resolution
At the end of each Age, after all cards in the structure have been played:
- Each player rolls two six-sided dice.
- Each player adds their current Summoning Value.
- Higher total wins the Summoning Resolution for that Age.

Formula:
Summoning Result = 2d6 + current Summoning Value

If tied:
Apply these tie-breakers in order:
1. Higher base Summoning Value wins.
2. If still tied, the player who did not start the current Age wins the ritual.

8.3 Reward for Winning a Summoning Resolution
If you win the Summoning Resolution of an Age:
- You gain 1 Divine Activation for EACH of your built Gods.
- You may resolve these activations immediately, in any order.
- If a God's power is persistent or repeatable, it is considered to have been activated once for that win.

Crucially:
- The powers of a God can be used as many times over the course of the game as the player wins Summoning Resolutions.
- If you built 3 Gods and win the Age II Summoning Resolution, you activate all 3 once.
- If you win again in Age III, each of those built Gods activates again once.

8.4 Optional Summoning Bonus Markers
To preserve the feel of crossing thresholds from the original military track, you may add 4 face-up Summoning Bonus markers to the board:
- Lesser Favor: opponent loses 2 coins
- Greater Favor: opponent loses 5 coins
- Sacred Omen: immediately gain 1 coin per built God
- Ritual Momentum: choose who begins next Age

Recommended trigger:
- The first player in the game to reach 3, 6, 9, and 12 total printed Summoning points claims one marker.
- Each marker can be claimed only once.

This is optional, but it helps red cards stay tense throughout the game.

--------------------------------------------------
9. START PLAYER FOR NEXT AGE
--------------------------------------------------
Because military track is removed, use this adapted rule:
- The player who WON the Summoning Resolution begins the next Age.
- Because the tie-break rules always determine a winner, each Age ends with one ritual victor.

This keeps ritual momentum tied to successful summoning.

--------------------------------------------------
10. DIVINE POWERS BY GOD
--------------------------------------------------
Balance principle:
- Tier A gods: strong tempo / removal / science swing
- Tier B gods: economy / flexible production / points
- Tier C gods: pressure / disruption / situational scaling

Each mythology is designed to have a spread of utility, tempo, economy, and point pressure.

--------------------------------
GREEK GODS
--------------------------------
Athena
Power: Gain 1 Progress token of your choice from those currently available on the board.
If none are available, gain 4 coins instead.
Notes: very strong strategic swing; equivalent to your proposal.

Zeus
Power: Destroy up to 2 Buildings of your opponent.
Restrictions for balance:
- They cannot be blue Guild cards (purple) or already built Gods.
- They may be any combination of brown, grey, yellow, red, blue, or green standard Buildings.
- Destroyed cards are discarded.
Notes: extremely strong disruption, therefore restricted from deleting guilds/gods.

Hera
Power: Take control of 1 eligible Building from your opponent.
Restrictions:
- You may steal a brown, grey, yellow, or blue card only.
- You cannot steal green, red, purple, or a built God.
- Move the card to your city; your opponent loses it.
Notes: stealing flexible utility is powerful but less explosive than Zeus.

Artemis
Power: Gain 1 resource of your choice for the current and future turns.
Mechanically: place a marker showing permanent production of exactly 1 chosen basic or manufactured resource.
Clarification:
- Choose one of Wood, Clay, Stone, Glass, or Papyrus when activated.
- This production does not affect the opponent's trading costs unless you choose to let all permanent production count normally. Recommended: it DOES count if it is a true produced resource.
Notes: stronger and cleaner than a one-shot resource.

Apollo
Power: Gain 5 coins from the bank.
Notes: simple economic deity; exactly as proposed.

--------------------------------
NORDIC GODS
--------------------------------
Balder
Power: Gain 3 victory points and 2 coins.
Notes: stable value god; rewards repeated wins without snowballing too hard.

Tyr
Power: Gain 2 Summoning points for the NEXT Summoning Resolution only.
Use a marker to remember this.
If activated during Age III, instead gain 4 victory points.
Notes: strong ritual war-god, but delayed and bounded.

Odin
Power: Look at the top 3 cards of the discard pile or, if using open discard, choose 1 discarded card and construct it for free.
Restrictions:
- You must still obey the 7-God cap; this cannot be used to build a God unless a separate rule allows it.
- If no suitable card exists, gain 4 coins instead.
Notes: mirrors wisdom/sacrifice and echoes mausoleum-like recovery without fully duplicating it.

Loki
Power: Copy the activated power of one of your other built Gods this Summoning Resolution.
Restrictions:
- Loki cannot copy Loki.
- If Loki is your only built God, gain 3 coins instead.
Notes: very high skill ceiling, but dependent on setup.

Thor
Power: Destroy 1 red card or 1 yellow card in your opponent's city. Then gain 1 coin.
If they have neither, gain 3 coins instead.
Notes: narrower than Zeus, but repeatable pressure on summoning/economy engines.

--------------------------------
AMERICAS GODS
--------------------------------
Huracán
Power: Your opponent loses 3 coins. You gain 3 coins.
If the opponent has fewer than 3 coins, they lose all remaining coins and you still gain 3.
Notes: strong swing, but less brutal than major removal.

Bochica
Power: Immediately gain 1 science symbol of your choice until end of game.
Use a Bochica marker to track it.
If this gives you 6 different scientific symbols, you win immediately.
Restriction:
- Bochica may only grant a symbol not already provided by Bochica before.
Notes: powerful, but tied to science lane.

Great Spirit
Power: Choose one:
- gain 1 flexible raw resource (Wood/Clay/Stone each turn), or
- gain 1 flexible manufactured resource (Glass/Papyrus each turn), or
- gain 3 victory points.
Flexible production from this power does not increase opponent trading costs.
Notes: broad spiritual versatility; one of the best balancing anchors in the set.

Quetzalcoatl
Power: Build one accessible card for free this turn, immediately after resolution.
Restrictions:
- This free build must be a Building, not a God.
- If no accessible card is available because the Age has ended, gain 5 coins instead.
Notes: strong tempo and chaining support.

Inti
Power: Gain 6 coins OR 4 victory points, your choice.
Notes: clean solar prosperity god; replaces Quetzalcoatl when selected out.

--------------------------------
MESOPOTAMIAN GODS
--------------------------------
Enki
Power: Gain 1 resource discount boon.
Choose one resource type among Wood, Clay, Stone, Glass, Papyrus.
For the rest of the game, you buy that resource from the bank for 1 coin each.
If you activate Enki again, you may choose a different resource type.
Notes: very strong long-term commerce god; excellent early, modest late.

Enlil
Power: Immediately reveal one face-down accessible-hidden card in the current Age structure, then gain 2 coins.
If no face-down card remains, gain 4 coins instead.
Notes: information plus tempo; subtle but powerful in skilled play.

Marduk
Power: Gain 2 victory points and choose one:
- gain 2 coins, or
- gain 1 Summoning point for the next Summoning Resolution only.
Notes: flexible king-god, tuned to be solid in every game state.

Anu
Power: Choose one unused Progress token from the box at random among 3 unseen tokens; keep 1 and return the others.
If no such tokens remain, gain 5 victory points instead.
Notes: echoes cosmic authority; strong but capped by token pool.

--------------------------------------------------
11. GOD COST TIERS (RECOMMENDED BALANCE)
--------------------------------------------------
To keep the build order and power curve balanced, classify gods into 3 construction tiers.

Tier I - easier to build, economy/flexibility gods
Suggested cost: any 2 resources OR 1 resource + 2 coins
Examples:
- Apollo
- Artemis
- Balder
- Great Spirit
- Enki
- Enlil

Tier II - medium strength, tactical swing gods
Suggested cost: 3 resources
Examples:
- Athena
- Tyr
- Huracán
- Inti
- Marduk
- Odin

Tier III - high-impact gods
Suggested cost: 4 resources, or 3 resources + 2 coins
Examples:
- Zeus
- Hera
- Loki
- Thor
- Bochica
- Quetzalcoatl
- Anu

Recommended constraint:
- A player may not build more than 2 Tier III gods in one game.
This avoids stacking too many explosive effects.

--------------------------------------------------
12. PROGRESS TOKENS - ADAPTED INTERACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------
All Progress tokens remain, but Strategy must be adapted.

Unchanged:
- Agriculture
- Architecture
- Economy
- Law
- Masonry
- Mathematics
- Philosophy
- Theology
- Urbanism

Adapted Strategy token:
Original military bonus is replaced.
New Strategy effect:
- Your future red cards provide +1 additional Summoning point.
- This does not affect red cards already built.
- This does not affect built Gods unless they explicitly grant Summoning points.

This preserves the timing logic of the original token.

--------------------------------------------------
13. GUILDS - ADAPTED INTERACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------
Most guilds remain unchanged.
Only the guild tied to red cards needs reinterpretation.

Unchanged:
- Builders Guild
- Moneylenders Guild
- Scientists Guild
- Shipowners Guild
- Traders Guild
- Magistrates Guild

Adapted Tacticians Guild:
- At construction: gain 1 coin for each red card in the city with the most red cards.
- Endgame: gain 1 victory point for each red card in the city with the most red cards.
Interpretation: red cards represent Ritual / Invocation institutions rather than military buildings.

--------------------------------------------------
14. END OF GAME AND SCORING
--------------------------------------------------
A game ends immediately if:
- a player reaches Scientific Supremacy, or
- a player achieves Cult Supremacy at the end of an Age.

If no supremacy occurs, the game ends after Age III and players total points.

Score:
- Victory points from Buildings
- Victory points from built Gods
- Victory points from Progress tokens
- Victory points from Guilds
- Treasury: each full set of 3 coins = 1 victory point
- Summoning bonus: recommended endgame bonus based on number of won Summoning Resolutions:
  - 0 wins: 0 VP
  - 1 win: 2 VP
  - 2 wins: 5 VP
  - 3 wins: 10 VP
This replaces the original military-track endgame score and preserves its scale.

Tiebreaker:
- player with most points from blue Buildings wins
- if still tied, shared victory

--------------------------------------------------
16. MECHANICAL CHANGES ADDED IN THIS ADAPTATION
--------------------------------------------------
Compared with the original duel structure, this variant adds or changes the following mechanics:

1. Wonder draft replaced by God draft.
2. Wonders replaced by cult construction of Gods.
3. Global cap of 7 Wonders replaced by global cap of 7 built Gods.
4. First player may build 4 Gods; second player may build only 3.
5. Military track removed entirely.
6. Shields converted into Summoning points on red cards.
7. End-of-Age Summoning Resolution added:
   - 2d6 + Summoning Value
8. Winning a Summoning Resolution grants Divine Activations for each built God.
9. Divine powers are repeatable across Ages whenever Summoning is won.
10. Start-player selection for the next Age now depends on losing the Summoning Resolution.
11. Strategy token adapted to buff future red cards as Summoning cards.
12. Optional threshold-based Summoning Bonus markers added to preserve tempo spikes from the original military zones.
13. New Cult Supremacy win condition added.
14. Endgame military scoring replaced by Summoning Resolution scoring.
15. God power tiers introduced to prevent removal/tempo gods from dominating the draft.

--------------------------------------------------
17. DESIGN NOTES FOR BALANCE
--------------------------------------------------
Recommended balancing decisions included here:
- Zeus cannot destroy guilds or gods.
- Hera cannot steal green, red, purple, or gods.
- Bochica grants strong science pressure but is Tier III and expensive.
- Loki is only strong if supported by other gods.
- Great Spirit and Enki are long-term value engines rather than explosive tempo.
- Tyr's bonus is delayed to avoid instant snowballing.
- Quetzalcoatl grants tempo but not God-construction tempo.
- Optional threshold markers are included to help red cards stay relevant throughout all 3 Ages.

--------------------------------------------------
19. IMPLEMENTED RULES OVERRIDES
--------------------------------------------------
The live Chaos Duel build currently uses these setup and ritual rules:
- The opening priority roll is 2d6 per player.
- The higher total chooses a mythology first.
- The lower total begins Age I.
- A mythology may be chosen by only one player.
- The next God a player builds requires 1, 2, 4, then 6 built Buildings.
- End-of-Age Summoning uses 2d6 + current Summoning Value.
- If totals tie, higher base Summoning Value wins.
- If still tied, the player who did not start the Age wins the ritual.
- The Summoning winner begins the next Age.
